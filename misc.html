MISC HOUSE RULES
  1. Rolling hit points
      Roll 3 hit dice and take the highest.

  2. Bennies
      Each player starts the very first session with one bennie. This bennie may be expended during play, converted for experience or saved for future sessions.

      Players earn bennies through roleplaying, mapping, running combat or keeping a log. These roles are assigned at the start of the campaign but may be changed if desired at a later date assuming all parties agree.

      Cartographer - A cartographer earns one bennie for each map he maintains during a session. Usually this applies for dungeons and overland hexes. Once a dungeon level is cleared, the mapper may earn an additional two bennies for redrawing the map and providing a key. Short notes about encounters and events may also be included on the map if desired, but not a complete log. This map will then be scanned at the next session and uploaded to the webpage for all players to reference as desired.

      Chronicler - A chronicler takes notes of the campaign. They are responsible for recording the narrative of play in a diary format. Who did what, when and where. A chronicler earns one bennie for maintaining a log during play. Converting the log to a narrative story for everyone to read, preferably in the format of a diary or short story will earn and additional two bennies. This may be typed or hand written and will be scanned for upload to the webpage when complete. Artistic drawings are optional, but would possibly earn more bennies depending on level of effort.

      Quartermaster - The quartermaster records all experience earned, tracks all wealth found and who ends up with what. They also record all equipment carried by each character and determines the resulting encumbrance. The quartermaster earns one bennie for maintaining this log during play. Converting the log to a libra office spread sheet in between sessions will earn the quartermaster an additional two bennies. This data will then be uploaded to the webpage for all players to view.

      Commander - The Commander runs combat during play. He tracks initiative and moves figures (including those run by the GM when needed) and generally assists with tactical game play. The Commander is reposible for recording marching order and nightly sleeping shifts of the party. The Commander earns two bennies per session.

      One Bennie May:
      1. Reroll one attack that missed in combat.
      2. Treat a fumble as a miss in combat.
      3. Reroll damage. Choose original or rerolled damage result.
      4. Prior to rolling d20, gain +5 bonus on one attack or skill attempt.
      5. Stabilize once hit points reduced to 0 or below.
      6. Cast a standard spell as a swift action.
      7. Cast a summoning spell as a standard action instead of a full round.
      8. Gain 1/4 total hit points as temporary hp +1/level.
      9. Heal 1/4 total hit points.
